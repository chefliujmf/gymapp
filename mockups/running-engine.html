Change one → the rest re-solve
Nudge Critical Speed or threshold pace and the whole curve shifts: D′, TTE, VDOT and every Daniels pace zone (E·M·T·I·R) recompute to stay consistent — one coupled model. (Max HR is separate — it moves your HR zones, not your paces.)
It reads your real runs. Tempo runs landing at ~5:28 confirm the threshold; a parkrun sets the VDOT; long runs anchor the CS floor. Nothing is invented — it's fitted to what you actually ran.
How it learns & adapts
Every run re-fits the curve — recent efforts weigh more, so it tracks your form. The band tightens as your efforts spread across distances and the signals — pace/HR decoupling, next-day HRV, workout execution — confirm. Terrain & GPS make pace noisier than power, so it leans on grade-adjusted pace + a wider honest band.
Same engine, both sports → one Overall
Identical maths to cycling (Critical Power ↔ Critical Speed, W′ ↔ D′). Your Overall profile rolls both engines together — VO₂max, TTE and form read across everything you do. Manual / Auto / Computed picker stays; Computed = this fit, and it never lowballs a value you train by.
