WHY "KIND" NOT DISTANCE

The KIND is what actually changes the plan — a race and a trip on the same date need OPPOSITE prep. That's the field that matters.

🏁
Race
Build → peak → taper. Race-specific intensity, then trim volume so you're fresh + fast on race day. Taper length scales with the demand (a 5K ~few days, an Ironman ~3–4 wks).
Chattanooga 70.3 · a crit · a marathon · a powerlifting meet
🧭
Trip / adventure
Build the specific fitness, DON'T taper. For Girona: ramp endurance + climbing durability so you can ride big days back-to-back and enjoy it — arrive fit, not peaked (you're not racing).
Girona cycling week · a hut-to-hut hike · a bikepacking trip
🏕️
Training camp
The camp IS the overload. Keep the days before manageable so you arrive fresh enough to absorb it, then program a real RECOVERY block after — don't stack hard work on top.
A training week away · a big volume block
📏
Test / benchmark
Freshen, then measure. A couple easy days into an FTP test / time trial / 1RM day so the number is real, not fatigue-masked. Feeds your benchmarks.
FTP test · CSS swim test · a 5K time trial

✓ Only 4 kinds, but they cover everything + each drives a genuinely different plan. Distance/demand is the FINE-tune on top; the KIND is the lever.
