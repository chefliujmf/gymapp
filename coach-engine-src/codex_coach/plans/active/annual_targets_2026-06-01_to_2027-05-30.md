## Feasibility, W/kg, and the revise-vs-chase gate

Frame the goal honestly. This protects trust and prevents chasing a number into a hole.

- **State the goal in W/kg, not just watts.** All-round and climbing performance is W/kg. At
  ~76 kg, 260 W ≈ 3.4 W/kg and 300 W ≈ 3.9 W/kg; at ~74 kg, 300 W ≈ 4.0 W/kg. The Sept 1 target is
  really "reach ~3.9–4.0 W/kg."
- **Two levers, not one.** That W/kg can come from raising FTP *or* from trimming mass healthily,
  or both. Example: 285 W at 72 kg ≈ 3.96 W/kg reaches the same place with a smaller raw-watt jump.
  Never pursue weight loss in a way that compromises fueling, recovery, or health (see
  `instructions_nutrition_recovery.md`); raw FTP is the primary lever.
- **Be honest about the odds.** 260 → 300 W is +15% FTP in ~3 months. For a 40-year-old with five
  years of structured training on 5–9 h/week, that is a *very aggressive stretch*, not a base case —
  trained riders typically gain ~5–10% per year. Treat 300 W by Sept 1 as a motivating ceiling to
  build toward, and a 6–18 month horizon as the realistic path. Say this plainly when relevant;
  do not quietly imply it is on track if the evidence says otherwise.

**Revise-vs-chase gate.** At the consolidation/recovery checkpoints (≈ week 6 Jul 6, week 10
Aug 3, week 12 Aug 17), compare the actual working FTP trajectory to the path 300 W would require:
- roughly on track (e.g. working FTP ≈ 280–290 W by early–mid August, recovery markers stable):
  continue the aggressive build into the Sept 1 validation;
- clearly behind, or markers/sleep/Form show poor absorption: **revise the Sept 1 target to a
  realistic value (e.g. the actual repeatable FTP + a small step), reset the validation as a
  checkpoint rather than a deadline, and keep building.** Do not add more overload to force it.

A revised target is a coaching success, not a failure — it keeps the athlete healthy and
progressing instead of overreaching for an arbitrary date.
