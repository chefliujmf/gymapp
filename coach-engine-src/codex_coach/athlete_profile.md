- motivation pattern: strongly goal-driven and responds well to clear progression toward higher FTP and all-round cycling strength

- communication preference: direct coach call first, rationale second, minimal menu of options
- accountability preference: wants specific actions, not vague labels such as `mobility`, `reset`, `stretch`, or `recover`

## Body maintenance

- left calf tendency: left calf can feel tight; interpret L/R balance alongside calf feel, cadence, torque, and fatigue rather than from one number alone
- default left-calf routine after easy rides or when tightness is mentioned: foam roller or massage gun 60-90 seconds per calf with light/moderate pressure, then straight-knee calf stretch 2 x 30 seconds per side, bent-knee soleus stretch 2 x 30 seconds per side, slow eccentric calf raises 2 x 8-10 per side with a 3-second lower, and tibialis raises 2 x 12-15
- stop rule: no sharp pain, nerve symptoms, Achilles pain, or next-day worsening; if those appear, stop the routine and reduce calf loading until assessed
