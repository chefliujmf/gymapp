### Cable Romanian Deadlift

Source:
- Centr tutorial: `https://centr.com/article/show/32423/power-shred-tutorial-cable-romanian-deadlift`

Category:
- strength

Movement pattern:
- hinge

Best use:
- gym strength maintenance;
- hinge slot when the athlete wants a Centr demo;
- lower-back-friendly alternative if cable setup feels better than barbell loading.

Default prescription:
- `3 x 6-8`
- rest `90-150 sec`
- effort `RPE 5-6`, keeping `2-3` reps in reserve

Substitutions:
- dumbbell Romanian deadlift;
- barbell Romanian deadlift;
- machine hinge if available and comfortable.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- stop or reduce load if hamstrings or low back feel strained;
- do not chase soreness before key bike sessions;
- skip hard finishers attached to the source workout.

These entries capture public JOIN workout ideas as source inspiration, then rewrite them into coach-owned patterns for Jean-Manuel. Do not copy JOIN workout text into planned workouts; use these adapted versions when they fit the current block.

### JOIN-Inspired High-Cadence Endurance

Source:
- JOIN public article: `https://join.cc/cycling-tips/beginner-cycling-training-plan`
- JOIN public workout category: `https://join.cc/workout-categories/endurance`

Category:
- cycling workout pattern
- endurance
- cadence skill

Movement pattern:
- aerobic endurance with relaxed high-cadence bias

Best use:
- easy endurance days under fatigue control;
- frequency rides when the goal is more riding, not more stress;
- outdoor endurance with light skill focus;
- after a hard day when cadence smoothness matters more than power.

Default prescription:
- duration `45-120 min` depending on availability and fatigue;
- power `50-72% FTP`;
- cadence mostly `85+ rpm`, no forced spin if terrain makes it awkward;
- effort `RPE 2-4`, conversational;
- allow short terrain-driven rises only if the ride stays mostly easy.

Substitutions:
- shorten to `30-45 min` recovery endurance if sleep, legs, or schedule are poor;
- add `3 x 3 min` at `95-100 rpm` only if it stays easy;
- replace with rest or mobility if easy riding does not feel easy.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- no tempo chasing;
- no low-cadence torque work if the left calf is tight;
- stop or shorten if RPE drifts above `4` for normal endurance power.

Athlete feedback:
- useful match for the athlete's current preference for riding more often while protecting key workouts.

### JOIN-Inspired Cadence Pyramid

Source:
- JOIN public workout: `https://join.cc/workouts/cycling-workouts/cadence-pyramid-0hours-45minutes`
- JOIN public article: `https://join.cc/cycling-tips/beginner-cycling-training-plan`

Category:
- cycling workout pattern
- neuromuscular skill

Movement pattern:
- high-cadence control with easy recoveries

Best use:
- short weekday skill ride;
- low-stress variety during build weeks;
- warmup skill block before endurance;
- technique day when intensity should stay controlled.

Default prescription:
- total duration `35-60 min`;
- warm up `10-15 min` easy;
- cadence ladder `1-2-3-2-1 min` at `100-105 rpm`;
- recover `1 min` easy between steps at natural cadence;
- keep power endurance-only, usually `50-70% FTP`;
- cool down easy by feel.

Substitutions:
- use `1-2-3-4-3-2-1 min` only when the day has enough room and low fatigue;
- lower cadence target to `95-100 rpm` outdoors if road conditions make higher cadence sloppy;
- split into `2 x 5 min` high-cadence blocks if the ladder is too fussy.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- this is coordination, not hidden intensity;
- stop the drill if hips rock, breathing rises sharply, or power climbs into tempo;
- avoid if left calf tightness appears with high cadence.

Athlete feedback:
- relevant because the May 22 sweet spot ride felt smoother at roughly `93-97 rpm`.

### JOIN-Inspired Sweet Spot With Seated Accelerations

Source:
- JOIN public workout: `https://join.cc/workouts/cycling-workouts/2-series-sweet-spot-with-accelerations-2hours-00minutes`
- JOIN public workout: `https://join.cc/workouts/cycling-workouts/8-min-sweet-spot-10-sec-seated-accelerations-1hours-01minutes`

Category:
- cycling workout pattern
- sweet spot
- seated acceleration

Movement pattern:
- sustained sub-threshold pressure with short controlled seated accelerations

Best use:
- after the athlete has shown sweet spot control;
- outdoor skill progression where power changes must stay smooth;
- all-round cycling development without FTP testing;
- future build weeks when Friday quality remains the anchor.

Default prescription:
- short version: `4 x 8 min` at `86-91% FTP`, `4 min` easy recoveries, then `4-6 x 10 sec` seated accelerations;
- longer version: `2 x 16-18 min` at `86-91% FTP`, include `10-20 sec` controlled seated accelerations inside or after each block;
- acceleration effort: fast leg speed and smooth torque, not a sprint;
- cap session at `RPE 6` unless explicitly planned harder.

Substitutions:
- remove accelerations if the left calf is tight or cadence control degrades;
- use plain sweet spot blocks when the training goal is FTP confidence rather than snap;
- shorten blocks to `3 x 10-12 min` if outdoor terrain interrupts execution.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- no standing sprinting;
- stop accelerations if they pull the whole workout into threshold feel;
- skip this the day after gym legs or poor sleep.

Athlete feedback:
- relevant after May 22 showed strong sweet spot control and good cadence discipline; not needed immediately if the next goal is endurance absorption.

### JOIN-Inspired 2x20 Intensive Endurance

Source:
- JOIN public workout: `https://join.cc/workouts/cycling-workouts/2x-20-min-intensive-endurance-intervals-2hours-00minutes`
- JOIN endurance category examples: `https://join.cc/workout-categories/endurance`

Category:
- cycling workout pattern
- sustained tempo
- durability

Movement pattern:
- long controlled tempo / intensive endurance intervals

Best use:
- durability work without threshold testing;
- 90-120 minute weekday-plus or weekend slot;
- bridge between easy endurance and harder over-under sessions.

Default prescription:
- total duration `75-120 min`;
- warm up `15-20 min`;
- `2 x 20 min` at `78-86% FTP`, recover `8-10 min` easy;
- cadence around natural efficient endurance cadence, usually `85-95 rpm`;
- cap at `RPE 5-6`.

Substitutions:
- `3 x 12-15 min` when terrain or time makes 20-minute blocks awkward;
- `1 x 30 min` steady tempo if the goal is pacing simplicity;
- reduce to endurance-only if RPE rises above the cap in the first block.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- no threshold creep;
- if the second interval is materially harder at the same power, finish endurance-only;
- do not stack near over-under or VO2 work without at least one easy day.

Athlete feedback:
- likely useful as a controlled FTP-building support workout once the current sweet spot and over-under checks are stable.

### JOIN-Inspired VO2-To-Threshold Combo

Source:
- JOIN public workout: `https://join.cc/workouts/cycling-workouts/2-min-vo2max-threshold-intervals-0hours-55minutes`

Category:
- cycling workout pattern
- VO2
- threshold finish

Movement pattern:
- short VO2 efforts followed by controlled threshold work

Best use:
- later build block after aerobic consistency and threshold control are stable;
- short weekday high-quality workout when freshness is high;
- not a recovery week or day-after-hard-session option.

Default prescription:
- total duration `50-65 min`;
- warm up thoroughly `15-20 min`;
- `4-6 x 2 min` at `105-115% FTP`, recover `2 min` easy;
- after recovery, `2 x 5 min` at `95-100% FTP`, recover `4-5 min`;
- stop before desperation; quality matters more than completing every rep.

Substitutions:
- start with `4 x 2 min` before progressing to `5-6`;
- replace threshold finish with tempo if VO2 reps are already costly;
- use indoor trainer only outside summer preference or if explicitly chosen.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- high recovery cost;
- skip if sleep, HRV/resting HR trend, legs, or life stress are poor;
- stop if cadence collapses, breathing becomes panicked, or power falls sharply.

Athlete feedback:
- relevant for future FTP ceiling work, but not current default while rebuilding rhythm after Girona.

### JOIN-Inspired 30/30 Repeatability

Source:
- JOIN public workout: `https://join.cc/workouts/cycling-workouts/2-sets-30-30s-2hours-30minutes`

Category:
- cycling workout pattern
- VO2 / anaerobic repeatability
- race-prep

Movement pattern:
- repeated hard 30-second efforts with equal recoveries

Best use:
- race-prep or punchy repeatability block;
- only after endurance base and threshold control are stable;
- not needed for the immediate all-round FTP build unless a specific event requires it.

Default prescription:
- starter version: `2 x 6-8 x 30 sec hard / 30 sec easy`, `6-8 min` easy between sets;
- hard means repeatable high power, not first-rep sprinting;
- use a generous warmup and cooldown;
- total duration `60-90 min` for Jean-Manuel before considering longer versions.

Substitutions:
- `3 x 5 min` over-under style if repeat sprintiness is not the priority;
- `8 x 1 min` controlled VO2 if the athlete needs less stochastic work;
- endurance-only if freshness is not clearly good.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- do not use close to key sweet spot/threshold work;
- stop when power drops, form gets ragged, or left calf/ankle complains;
- avoid if the goal is steady FTP validation.

Athlete feedback:
- save for later. Interesting, but not well matched to the current controlled build unless the plan shifts toward punchy event preparation.

## Prehab And Body Maintenance

### Left-Calf Maintenance Routine

Source:
- coach prescription from athlete profile and workout-analysis instructions.

Category:
- prehab
- recovery
- body maintenance

Movement pattern:
- calf, soleus, ankle, tibialis

Best use:
- after easy rides;
- when left calf tightness is mentioned;
- during lower-load weeks to keep the routine familiar.

Default prescription:
- foam roller or massage gun: `60-90 sec per calf`, light to moderate pressure;
- straight-knee calf stretch: `2 x 30 sec per side`;
- bent-knee soleus stretch: `2 x 30 sec per side`;
- slow eccentric calf raise: `2 x 8-10 per side`, `3 sec` lower;
- tibialis raise: `2 x 12-15`.

Substitutions:
- omit eccentric raises if symptoms worsen during the set;
- use both legs up and one leg down if single-leg raises are too hard.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- stop if pain rises during the routine or changes walking mechanics;
- keep pressure moderate, not bruising;
- if symptoms persist or escalate, treat it as a plan-adjustment signal.

### Desk Cyclist Hip/Glute Reset

Source:
- coach adaptation from `Vegetarian Cyclist Performance Optimization Plan.pdf`.

Category:
- prehab
- mobility
- activation
- body maintenance

Movement pattern:
- hip flexor mobility
- glute activation
- trunk control

Best use:
- after long desk days;
- before gym when hips feel locked up;
- after easy rides if low back, hip flexors, or glutes feel inactive;
- as a non-fatiguing support routine on recovery days.

Default prescription:
- light hip-flexor/quad release: `30-60 sec per side`, gentle pressure only;
- half-kneeling hip-flexor stretch with posterior pelvic tilt: `2 x 45-60 sec per side`;
- glute bridge: `2 x 10-15`, pause 1 sec at the top;
- clamshell: `2 x 15-20 per side`, slow and controlled;
- dead bug: `2 x 6-8 per side`, low back quiet against the floor.

Desk micro-dose:
- stand or walk `2-5 min` each hour when practical;
- use `5-10` easy glute squeezes after long seated blocks;
- avoid turning desk breaks into training.

Substitutions:
- use side plank instead of dead bug if trunk control is the priority;
- use banded lateral walks only if they do not create hip flexor or glute soreness before a key ride;
- skip the release step if it feels awkward or provocative.

Cycling cost and stop rules:
- no aggressive pressure near the abdomen, groin, pulse points, or nerve symptoms;
- stop if pain, numbness, tingling, pinching, or next-day worsening appears;
- keep the routine easy enough that it improves motion without adding leg fatigue.
