This is the coach's living cookbook for meals, snacks, and recipe templates that support the cycling plan.

Entries should be practical, repeatable, vegetarian-compatible where possible, and tied to a training use case. Source links identify the origin; the coach guidance is written in original wording.

## Centr Recipes

### Swirled Banana & Oat Pots

Source:
- Centr recipe: `https://centr.com/recipe/show/32848/swirled-banana-oat-pots`

Category:
- pre-ride breakfast
- carb-forward snack
- rest-day breakfast if portion is matched to appetite

Best use:
- easy ride under 75 minutes when starting hungry;
- endurance ride when eaten early enough before the session;
- quality ride only if digestion is reliable and there is enough lead time.

Training purpose:
- provides a simple carbohydrate base with enough substance to avoid starting underfed.

Vegetarian suitability:
- suitable if prepared with vegetarian-compatible ingredients.

Coach adaptation:
- for rides longer than 90 minutes, pair with planned on-bike carbs rather than making the bowl excessively large;
- for late sessions, use a smaller serving and add a simple carb snack closer to the ride if needed;
- if GI heaviness appears, reduce fat/fiber additions before changing the workout fuel target.

Category:
- recovery snack
- post-gym convenience option

- after hard or long rides when dinner will be delayed;

- protein powder is optional and should be treated as convenience, not a requirement.

## Book-Derived Coach Templates

These are original coach templates built from sports nutrition principles in the knowledge base. They are not copied recipes.

### Carb-Forward Oat Bowl Template

Source inspiration:
- `Fuel Your Ride`
- `The Complete Guide to Sports Nutrition`
- `Plant-based Sports Nutrition`
- `The Feed Zone Cookbook`

Category:
- pre-ride breakfast
- pre-quality-session meal

Best use:
- 2-4 hours before sweet spot, threshold, or endurance rides;
- breakfast before a long ride when normal digestion is reliable.

Coach-created base:
- oats or another familiar grain;
- banana, berries, maple syrup, jam, or another simple carbohydrate;
- yogurt, soy yogurt, milk, soy milk, or protein powder if more protein is needed;
- small amount of nuts, seeds, or nut butter only if it digests well before riding.

Training purpose:
- arrive at the session with carbohydrate availability high enough to execute quality work.

Coach adaptation:
- keep fat and fiber moderate before hard sessions;
- use a smaller portion plus a simple snack if the ride starts soon;
- use a larger portion only when there is enough lead time and the ride is long.

Athlete feedback:
- not logged yet.

### Protein-Forward Recovery Smoothie Template

Source inspiration:
- `Fuel Your Ride`
- `The Complete Guide to Sports Nutrition`
- `Plant-based Sports Nutrition`

Category:
- recovery snack
- post-gym protein option
- post-ride bridge meal

Best use:
- after gym;
- after hard bike sessions when a full meal is delayed;
- after evening training when a heavy meal could hurt sleep.

Coach-created base:
- milk, soy milk, or yogurt base;
- banana, dates, oats, or berries depending on carbohydrate need;
- protein powder only if normal food is not practical;
- cocoa, cinnamon, coffee, or salt for flavor as tolerated.

Training purpose:
- provide protein after strength work and add carbohydrate when the session was long, hard, or close to the next workout.

Coach adaptation:
- after gym only: protein-forward is enough unless the next day has bike work;
- after long or hard ride: add carbohydrate deliberately;
- if appetite is low, split recovery into a smoothie now and normal meal later.

Athlete feedback:
- used in activity recovery trace `2026-05-21 - Thursday Easy Spin` as the coach-owned fallback; athlete feedback not logged yet.
